About Freemasonry

What is Freemasonry?

Freemasonry is the world's oldest fraternity. We welcome upstanding men of any religion, race, country, career, or political background. Freemasonry is different in every place and country, but we are brought together by an incredible shared experience.

The world needs dire help, but there’s not much that we can do about that on the macro scale. Freemasonry gives men the opportunity to change and improve themselves. We do this by providing men with moral tools and teachings centered around love of God and love of one another. These tools and teachings help them better live by their existing moral values. Through this process, a man not only changes himself, but the world immediately around him. Sometimes, he can change the course of a whole country. We can’t change the world on a macro scale, but we can change it many, many times on a micro scale. Freemasonry is an organized effort to save the world through love, one man at a time.

What isn't Freemasonry?

What isn't Freemasonry?

Freemasonry is not:

  • a religion. Freemasons are required to believe in God, but how we choose to believe in God is up to each individual. We are also prohibited from discussing religion while in lodge. Because of this, brethren of all religions can be found happily sitting in lodge together.
  • a political party. Freemasons come from all sorts of political backgrounds, and, just like in the case of our religious beliefs, we are prohibited from discussing politics while in lodge.
  • a therapy group. We are here to help good men get better, but Freemasonry is not, and cannot be, a substitute for professional mental health counseling.
  • a charity. Freemasons are certainly charitable, and Freemasons collectively contribute over $3 million to charity per day in the United States, but we are not just a charity.
