Is Freemasonry For You?

Freemasonry is a powerful initiatic tradition focused on self-improvement through brotherhood, regardless of race, creed, or religion. However, not every person is right for Freemasonry, and not every person is right for every lodge. There are a number basic prerequisites you must meet to become a Freemason in Texas.

  • You must be male.
  • You must not be a felon.
  • You must believe in:
    • a Supreme Being (God),
    • the immortality of the human soul,
    • and the holy writings of your religion.

The New Candidate Process

As Fort Worth Lodge, we have a strong focus on member safety and lodge security. Throughout the process of becoming a member, your good character, demeanor, and personality will be a focus of our investigation. Assuming the lodge approves you to proceed at each step, the membership process is as follows:

  • Establish first contact via email with the lodge and fill out a prospect survey.
  • Attend our monthly New Candidate Night to ask questions and get to know some of our members.
  • Begin attending monthly stated meeting dinners and regular fellowship sessions as a prospect.
  • Receive a petition (application) from the lodge, fill it out, and turn it in.
  • Undergo three formal interviews by the lodge investigation committee.
  • Be balloted (voted) on at a stated meeting.
  • Receive your Entered Apprentice degree and begin your masonic journey.

The membership process is designed for the members of Fort Worth Lodge to get to know you better and feel full confidence when balloting on your petition for the degrees of Freemasonry. This process necessitates regular attendance as a prospect for five months or more, as needed.

To take the first step towards membership, email us here.

Vision & Mission

Our vision is to be known as a masonic lodge that ensures the advancement of Freemasonry and the quality of the masonic experience.

Our mission is to initiate well-qualified men, provide quality masonic education, and engage all members in continuing fellowship and activities.

Meetings

Our stated meetings are on the second Monday of every month, except for holidays. Dinner is at six o' clock, and the meeting starts at seven o' clock. Dress is business casual or better.

Our called meetings are on most Mondays at half-past six o' clock. Degrees will be advertised via our mailing list. Dress is business casual or better for attendees, and "full blackout" (black suit, black tie, black shirt, black footwear) for degree cast members.

If you need any information regarding our meetings, email us here.

Degree Fees & Dues

All prices are in USD.

Fort Worth Lodge dues are $365 per year. Enrollment in a quarterly dues program is available as well. Degree fees are as follows:

  • Entered Apprentice - $250
  • Fellowcraft - $100
  • Master Mason - $100

To offset the cost of online banking, each dues payment or degree fee is accompanied by a processing fee. If you would like to forgo this additional fee, you may pay your dues in cash or by check.

Endowments

Purchasing an endowment from Fort Worth Lodge costs $3700. This is a serious commitment, and before taking it on, we ask that you meet with the officers of the lodge. If you are interested in purchasing an endowment, please contact us by email or in person to see if an endowment is right for you.
